Our top development comes from UN News, reporting an alarming trend in cyberbullying. A recent poll indicates two-thirds of children globally have witnessed an increase in online harassment, with half feeling unsupported. This surge is significantly driven by artificial intelligence, which is making cyberbullying faster, more targeted, and harder to detect, particularly through sophisticated deepfakes. The UN stresses an urgent, ecosystem-wide collaboration is needed to shield children from these evolving digital harms.

Also from UN News, a stark report details the brutal reality of gang recruitment in Haiti. Children are being lured by gangs with promises of money and status, only to be trapped by violence and death threats if they attempt to leave or disobey. The UN highlights the escalating security and humanitarian crisis, with 1.4 million people displaced and thousands dead due to gang violence, underscoring the critical need for robust child protection measures in the nation.

Moving to the Horn of Africa, UN News reports a positive development in South Sudan. The UN Mission in South Sudan, UNMISS, is deploying mobile courts in Western Equatoria State. This initiative aims to tackle a severe backlog of justice cases, including murder and sexual violence, and reinforce the rule of law. For communities long affected by instability, these mobile courts represent a crucial step towards delivering long-delayed justice and accountability.

In a deeply concerning global trend, UN News reports that women's rights are regressing worldwide. A UN Women report warns of a significant increase in gender-based violence and a widening "justice gap," exacerbated by rising conflicts. The report highlights that women globally possess only 64% of the legal rights afforded to men, with an alarming 87% increase in conflict-related sexual violence violations. It calls for urgent judicial reforms designed by and for women to combat systemic failures and discriminatory laws.

ACTIONABLE INTELLIGENCE:
This regression in women's rights is flagged as actionable intelligence, indicating a critical area for policy intervention and monitoring by international bodies and NGOs focused on human rights and gender equality.

Continuing on the theme of women's rights, UN News covered International Women's Day and the opening of the 70th session of the Commission on the Status of Women. UN Secretary-General António Guterres reiterated that women globally hold only 64% of men's legal rights, urging the dismantling of discriminatory laws. Particularly concerning are reports from Afghanistan, where women and girls face systematic erasure from public life and education under Taliban rule, a situation highlighted by figures like Malala Yousafzai.
